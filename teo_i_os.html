      PRIME COST

      It Is the result of adding the raw material and the cost of the direct labour.
      To process the raw material, personnel are required to handle and process the raw material directly; Is what is known asDirect Labour.

      Raw Material; They are the materials that will be subjected to transformation or manufacturing operations for their physical and/or chemical change, before they can be sold as finished products.


      Cost of production

      The cost of production is expenses that an organization incurs for the development of a project. That Is to say from the main material to the manufacturing process, also called " Processing cost ".

      Is the result of adding prime cost more direct manufacturing Overhead more the indirect cost.


      INDIRECT COSTS

      What characterizes these costs is that they cannot be allocated directly to each of the products. It will Be done only after having defined a coherent allocation criterion, which allows you to calculate your assignment to each product individually, which will guide you later when defining the final price of sale.

      Indirect costs are equal to: indirect raw Material more direct Labor more indirect manufacturing overhead


      DISTRIBUTION FEED

      They refer to the expenditures (expenses and investments) that companies make to distribute their products in the market.

      Distribution costs are conformed by marketing and financing costs. They Include the costs of packing, special packaging, storage, transfer of the merchandise, those generated by the payment of the personnel charged for the sale of the product, of administration and the decrease. The depletion is the loss of a physical or material good.
